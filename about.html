Were Building

The founding essay. Why Docs Africa exists and what it is building toward.

The current coverage treats
African builders like data points.

Funding rounds. Valuations. X raises M to solve Y problem in Z market. The person disappears inside the headline. The obsession, the sacrifice, the specific genius of how their mind works — gone. You get the what. You never get the who.

And the who is everything.

"Nobody knew Rihanna when she was just a little gyal on the island. She got discovered."

Docs Africa discovers the tech giants building Africa — and in doing so, discovers Africa. We are not a media company. We are not a blog with good photography. We are a discovery engine — the mechanism by which African builders go from building in the dark to being known by the world.

The photoshoot is the draft call. The profile is the Vogue cover. The award night is the ceremony where the world officially acknowledges what was always true. When Docs Africa finds you, your story stops being invisible.

Fifty years from now, someone will go looking for what it actually felt like to build in Lagos in 2024. In Aba. In Nairobi. In Accra. Most of what exists will be press releases and pitch decks. Docs Africa exists because that is not acceptable.

Every builder we document is a permanent record. Every profile is proof that this generation was here, was building, and was extraordinary. The archive doesn't need happy endings. It needs the truth. That's what makes it last.

01
Discovery First

We find the builders. They do not find us. There is no application, no submission portal, no form. If you are building something that matters, Docs Africa is already looking.

02
The Full Truth

We document the full story — not just the wins. The pivots, the failures, the cost of building. The archive is honest because half-truths are not history.

03
Africa Is The Aesthetic

We do not impose a visual language on the continent. The builders, the locations, the specific texture of building here — that is the aesthetic. Africa is not the decoration. Africa is the content.

04
Legends, Not Press Releases

Every builder we document deserves the full treatment. The profile, the film, the photoshoot, the interview. We do not publish press releases dressed as profiles. We make legends.
